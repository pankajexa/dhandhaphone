# Indian Festival and Business Calendar

Festivals are the biggest driver of retail spending in India. Stock-up typically starts 2-4 weeks before the festival. Credit extensions are common pre-festival; collections peak post-festival.

## Pan-Indian Festivals

### Diwali / Deepavali (Oct-Nov)
- **Business impact:** HIGHEST spending period of the year
- **Stock up:** Sweets, dry fruits, decorations, electronics, appliances, gold, clothing
- **Credit pattern:** Heavy credit extension 2-3 weeks before; collect aggressively after
- **Note:** Corporate gifting peaks; bulk orders from offices

### Holi (Mar)
- **Business impact:** Moderate. Colors, sweets, beverages
- **Stock up:** Gulal, pichkari, white clothing, thandai ingredients, bhang
- **Credit pattern:** Short cycle, mostly cash/UPI

### Makar Sankranti / Pongal / Lohri (Jan 13-15)
- **Business impact:** Regional but significant. Kite-selling in Gujarat, new clothes in South
- **Stock up:** Kites, thread (manja), til-gul, sugarcane, new pots

### Raksha Bandhan (Aug)
- **Stock up:** Rakhis, sweets, gift items, chocolates
- **Business impact:** Good for gifting, sweets, and garment shops

### Independence Day / Republic Day (Aug 15 / Jan 26)
- **Business impact:** Low commercial. Flags, badges, school supplies

### New Year (Jan 1) / Financial Year Start (Apr 1)
- **Business impact:** Stock clearance sales in Dec-Jan. New accounting year starts Apr 1

## Tax and Compliance Calendar

| Date | Event | Action Required |
|------|-------|-----------------|
| **11th of every month** | GSTR-1 due | File outward supply return |
| **20th of every month** | GSTR-3B due | File summary return + pay tax |
| **18th after quarter** | CMP-08 due | Composition scheme quarterly payment |
| **25th of month 1,2** | PMT-06 | QRMP monthly tax payment |
| **31st March** | Financial year end | Close books, stocktake, reconcile |
| **30th April** | GSTR-4 due | Composition annual return |
| **31st July** | ITR filing deadline | Income tax return (non-audit) |
| **30th September** | ITR (audit cases) | Tax audit entities |
| **31st October** | ITR (transfer pricing) | International transactions |
| **31st December** | GSTR-9 due | Annual GST return |

## Planning Tips

- **Stock up 3-4 weeks** before major regional festivals
- **Extend credit cautiously** pre-Diwali; set clear repayment dates
- **Hire temporary help** for Diwali, Onam, Durga Puja peaks
- **Clear old inventory** during off-season (Jan-Feb, Jun-Jul) with discounts
- **Track regional events** -- a festival in one state may not matter in another
- **Wedding season overlap** (Nov-Feb) amplifies festive demand
